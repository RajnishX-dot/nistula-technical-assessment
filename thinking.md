### A. Reply you’d send at 3am

Hi, this is Nistula. Sorry you’re without hot water, especially with guests coming. I’ve pinged the night caretaker and duty manager so someone can look at the geyser and mains. I can’t approve refunds from this chat; I’ve forwarded your note and booking details to a manager. If you smell burning, see sparks, or anything feels unsafe, say so right away.

(Kept it short, didn’t promise money I can’t sign off on, gave a real escalation path.)

### B. What the platform should do, not just the chat bubble

Open a high-severity incident on the booking + villa + thread. SMS/push the caretaker, notify the duty manager, copy property ops on email so it isn’t one person’s memory. Log last boiler work, vendor SLA, tickets. If nobody’s on site inside the window you agreed with ops, book the plumber from the approved list. If there’s another room nearby, offer it. If nothing’s acknowledged in 30 minutes, roll to backup on-call, post a “still on it, next update in ~15m” line in the thread, and page whoever owns after-hours incidents. Store whether each outbound was sent from a draft as-is, edited draft, or typed from scratch.

### C. Third hot-water ticket in two months on the same villa

Stop treating it as random noise. Tag the pattern on that property, open a proper repair ticket after the third hit in a rolling window, and don’t let guest-facing auto-replies promise hot water is “sorted” until a vendor signs the job off. Give hosts a one-page checklist before the next check-in. If you can, do a quick hot-water check the morning of arrival and log it; flag vendor SLA misses on a dashboard so the next guest isn’t the first to find out.
